## How to Add Affiliate Links (Without Being Spammy)

### âœ… **DO:**

**1. Be transparent:**
- Add disclosure: "This post contains affiliate links. If you sign up, I may earn a commission at no cost to you. I only recommend products I use or have researched."
- Include in footer of every post with affiliate links
- FTC compliance (legally required)

**2. Add value first:**
- Explain WHY you recommend it
- Compare options (not just one affiliate link)
- Share your personal experience or plan to use it

**3. Make it natural:**
- Example: "When I transition my portfolio to index funds, I'm planning to use M1 Finance because of their automated rebalancing and tax-loss harvesting features. [Open an M1 Finance account here](#)."

**4. Use text links in context:**
- Link relevant keywords ("Personal Capital wealth tracker")
- Not: "Click here!!!" or "Best offer!!!"

### âŒ **DON'T:**

**1. Don't be pushy:**
- Avoid: "YOU MUST SIGN UP NOW!"
- Avoid: "This is the ONLY option!"

**2. Don't hide affiliate status:**
- Legally required to disclose
- Damages trust if people find out later

**3. Don't recommend crap:**
- Only promote what you'd genuinely recommend
- Your reputation > short-term commission

**4. Don't overdo it:**
- Max 3-5 affiliate links per blog post
- Focus on quality over quantity

## Legal Requirements (FTC Compliance)

**You MUST disclose affiliate relationships:**

**Add this to every post with affiliate links:**
```markdown
**Disclosure:** This post contains affiliate links. If you sign up through my links, I may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. I only recommend products I've researched or use myself. This helps support Fire Driven Media at no cost to you.
```

**Add to About page:**
- "Fire Driven Media participates in affiliate programs and may earn commissions on qualifying purchases."

**Where to disclose:**
- At top or bottom of blog post
- Near the affiliate link itself (best practice)
- In site footer (site-wide disclosure)

**Why it matters:**
- Legally required by FTC
- Builds trust (transparency)
- Protects you from fines ($43,792 per violation)

## Tracking & Optimization

**Monitor these metrics:**

1. **Click-through rate (CTR):**
   - How many visitors click affiliate links
   - Goal: 2-5% of visitors

2. **Conversion rate:**
   - How many clicks become signups
   - Goal: 10-30% (depends on offer)

3. **Earnings per visitor:**
   - Total affiliate revenue / total visitors
   - Goal: $0.10-0.50 per visitor (FIRE niche)

4. **Top-performing content:**
   - Which posts drive most affiliate revenue
   - Double down on what works

**Tools:**
- Each affiliate program has a dashboard
- Google Analytics (track outbound link clicks)
- Pretty Links plugin (if using WordPress) or custom link tracking
