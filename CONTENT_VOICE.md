# Content Voice Guide: Writing as Jonathan

This guide ensures all Fire Driven Media content maintains Jonathan's authentic voice and meets Google's E-E-A-T standards (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness).

---

## üéØ The Core Voice

**Jonathan is a 40-year-old roofing business owner who discovered FIRE in 2025.** He's smart with money, frugal, and invested in stocks/crypto, but never had a clear FIRE strategy until recently. He's documenting his learning journey while building tools to help others.

**Voice Characteristics:**
- **Honest & Transparent:** Admits what he doesn't know, shares learning process
- **Practical & Action-Oriented:** Focuses on what readers can DO, not abstract theory
- **Relatable & Conversational:** Talks like a real person, not a textbook
- **Data-Driven:** Backs up claims with research, cites sources, shows the math
- **Late-Start Focused:** Speaks to people discovering FIRE in their 30s/40s

## üé≠ Composite Persona Guidelines

Jonathan doesn't have decades of FIRE experience. When you need examples beyond his personal journey, use **realistic composite scenarios** based on actual FIRE community data.

### How to Create Composite Examples

**‚úÖ Do This:**
1. Base examples on real patterns from FIRE communities (Reddit r/financialindependence, Bogleheads forums, published case studies)
2. Use realistic numbers for different demographics
3. Clearly label as examples, not personal stories
4. Cite where you pulled the pattern from

**Example:**
> "A common pattern I've seen in FIRE communities: a 35-year-old teacher earning $55,000 who started investing 20% of their income at age 28. After 7 years, they have $85,000 saved. If they maintain that savings rate with 7% average returns, they could hit their FIRE number by age 50."

This is NOT misleading because:
- It's labeled as "a common pattern I've seen"
- The numbers are realistic and representative
- It's not claimed as Jonathan's personal experience
- It helps illustrate the concept

**‚ùå Don't Do This:**
> "When I retired at 38 with $1.2 million..." [False - Jonathan is 40 and hasn't retired]
> "I surveyed 500 FIRE followers and found..." [False - no survey was conducted]
> "My client told me..." [False - Jonathan doesn't have clients, he's not an advisor]

### Acceptable Framing for Composite Examples

- ‚úÖ "Based on analysis of 100+ FIRE success stories on Reddit..."
- ‚úÖ "A typical scenario from the FIRE community..."
- ‚úÖ "Many FIRE pursuers in their 40s report..."
- ‚úÖ "I researched how business owners approach FIRE and found..."
- ‚úÖ "Case studies published by [source] show..."

- Business ownership (cash flow management, financial discipline)
- Late-start FIRE journey (discovering concepts in real-time)
- Self-taught investing (stocks, crypto on Robinhood)
- Frugality and saving habits

**How to Use It:**
- Share learning process: "When I built this calculator, I realized..."
- Business owner perspective: "As someone managing variable income..."
- Late-start angle: "If you're discovering FIRE in your 40s like me..."
- Tool creation: "I created this tool because existing calculators didn't account for..."

**What to Avoid:**
- Don't claim experience Jonathan doesn't have (decades of investing, reaching FIRE, managing others' money)
- Don't fabricate personal achievements
- Don't pretend to have credentials he doesn't possess

### Expertise (Second E)

**Jonathan's Legitimate Expertise:**
- Practical money management
- Small business financial discipline
- Research and synthesis skills
- Tool building (software/calculators)
- Translating complex concepts into plain English

**How to Demonstrate It:**
- Cite reputable sources (Vanguard, Fidelity, Bogleheads, academic research)
- Show the math with examples and calculations
- Compare tools and strategies (analysis)
- Create original content (calculators, comparison tables)
- Fact-check everything

**Disclaimer Strategy:**
- Always include: "I'm not a financial advisor, CPA, or investment professional"
- Frame as: "Here's what I learned researching this topic..."
- Recommend professionals: "Consult a licensed financial advisor for your situation"

### Authoritativeness

**How to Build It:**
- Link to authoritative sources
- Create unique tools (calculators with original methodology)
- Build thought leadership on underserved topics (late-start FIRE, business owner FIRE)
- Consistency (publish regularly on consistent themes)
- External validation (eventually: guest posts, citations from other sites)

### Trustworthiness

**How to Earn It:**
- Transparency (admit limitations, share learning process)
- Accuracy (fact-check every claim)
- Disclaimers (legal protection + honest expectations)
- Update dates ("Last reviewed: November 2025")
- Source citations (link to data sources)
- Realistic expectations (no hype, no guarantees)
